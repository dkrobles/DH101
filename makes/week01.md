## Process Notes
How did you make this?

1. What Is Being Made?
What is the output of this project?
 (Map, image, network, visualization, text, interface, etc.)
The output of this project is a new generated image of a human that doesn’t exist. 
What experience does it create for the user?
I think it could create an eerie experience for the user because it shows we have the technology to create images of people that don’t exist, and that the quality is as good as it is.

2. What Is the Project Made From? (Data)
What counts as “data” here?
The data here are the images and conditions necessary to generate each face
Where does it come from?
The conditions are based off of an AI that was trained to study human faces and generate an image of “a person” accordingly with random conditions every time.
What seems missing or excluded?
What seems missing is real identity, personal history, and social context. The faces have no names, cultures, stories, or lived experiences attached to them.

3. Tools, Algorithms, or Systems
What software, platforms, or models are involved?
The project uses machine learning systems, specifically a generative adversarial network(GAN), along with web servers and AI frameworks that run the model and generate images in real time. 
Is AI used? If yes, where in the process?
Yes, AI is used for learning what human faces look like from large datasets and then generating entirely new faces based on those learned patterns.
What steps feel automated vs. guided?
The image generation feels fully automated since each face is created by the model without human input. The guided steps happened earlier in the process, such as training the model, selecting the data, designing the system, which required human planning and decisions.

4. Human Labor & Decisions
Who likely collected the data?
Researchers, developers, and organizations likely collected the original face images, often through large online datasets or scraped public sources.
Who cleaned, categorized, or interpreted it?
Data scientists and engineers would have cleaned the datasets, removed unusable images, standardized formats, and prepared the data for training the AI model.
Where do you see human judgment shaping the outcome?
Human judgement shaped the outcome in deciding what data to include, how the model was trained, how realistic the images should be, and how the website presents the results.

5. Design as Argument
How do visual or interface choices shape meaning?
The extremely simple interface(just a face on a blank page) makes the image feel more real and unsettling. 
What does the project make easy to see?
It makes it easy to see how realistic AI generated humans can be and how convincing synthetic images have become.
What does it make hard to see?
It makes it hard to see the data sources, the labor behind the system, the ethics of data collection, and the technological complexity involved in creating the images.


## Reflection
Respond to this week’s reflection prompt in 200–300 words.

In this project, “making” does not mean creating something by hand, but building a system that can generate things automatically. The humans involved are not making individual images; instead, they are making the machine, the model, and the structure that allows images to be produced endlessly. The act of making shifts from direct creation to system design. This changes the definition of authorship, because no single person can claim ownership over a specific face – it is created by an algorithm using learned patterns from data. 

When machines participate in making, creativity becomes distributed between humans and technology. Humans design the rules, gather the data, and build the systems, while machines execute the production. This creates distance between the creator and the final product, which can make the process feel less personal and more abstract. It also raises ethical questions about consent, data ownership, and identity, since real human faces are used to train systems that generate fake ones. The project shows how AI can produce realistic outputs without understanding meaning, identity, or humanity. Instead of making people, the machine makes representations of people. This changes making from an expressive human act into a technical process, where realism can exist without reality, and creation can happen without intention, emotion, or lived experience.
