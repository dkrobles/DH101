## Reflection
Respond to this week’s reflection prompt in 200–300 words.

Network Analysis:
The most connected node in this network is “On the Dangers of Stochastic Parrots,” a highly talked-about publication that discusses criticisms of large language models(LLM’s) and their social implications. In the visualization, this publication holds the highest degree of centrality since it connects various researchers, debates, and institutions.

There are two main clusters in this network. The first cluster consists of academic ethics research, including researchers like Kate Crawford and institutions like AI Now Institute and ACM FAccT Conference. These nodes all discuss bias, accountability, and social implications of AI systems. The second cluster consists of corporate-level AI development, including OpenAI, Google, and Microsoft. These nodes are connected through various forms of research funding, governance partnerships, and technical work on AI models.

The network also illustrates the tension between the concept of AI safety and the concept of AI ethics, as corporate labs focus on long-term safety and alignment issues, while academic critics discuss labor conditions, bias, and environmental issues. The visualization illustrates the connections between these two concepts through conferences and policy organizations.

Interestingly, the visualization also illustrates the importance of “bridging” actors, such as Joy Buolamwini, as she brings academic research and activism together through her Algorithmic Justice League, indicating high betweenness centrality.

However, the visualization also shows the absence of many actors in the network. Data laborers, gig workers, and communities affected by AI technologies are largely absent from the visualization. While they are important to the functioning of AI technologies, they are not often cited in academic literature or visible in conferences. The absence of these actors is an important example of the ways in which visualizing social networks can reinforce existing power structures, as the most visible and well-represented actors are often the ones with the most power and resources.

Artist Statement: 

This network visualization aims to show how proper works in current discussions around artificial intelligence ethics. It shows relationships between researchers, companies, publications, and organizations, giving us insight into who has visibility and power in current discussions around AI ethics.

At the center of this network is the academic paper “On the Dangers of Stochastic Parrots.” Its position at the center of this network illustrates how academic literature can be a means of connecting individuals, institutions, and corporations.

At the same time, corporations like OpenAI and Google have high connectedness in this network due to their control over infrastructure, funding, and computational resources for large-scale artificial intelligence projects. These corporations’ high connectedness is an example of how power works in the artificial intelligence world: corporations with more computational resources often have more control over the direction of artificial intelligence.

However, it is interesting to see what is missing in this network. For example, workers who label data for artificial intelligence models, communities affected by surveillance technology, and environmental limitations of this method of visualization can only show data that can be collected, often from sources that have been considered authoritative.

If the network were organized differently, for instance, along the lines of supply chains or labor relationships rather than citations, it would have very different implications for the story that is being told about the power of AI. Such a network would probably place miners, data workers, and infrastructure providers at the center, rather than researchers and corporations.

In this sense, the visualization is not just describing reality; it is also constructing reality.
