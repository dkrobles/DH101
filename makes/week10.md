## Reflection
Respond to this week’s reflection prompt in 200–300 words.

Design Statement: 

In The Perfect Candidate, which is an interactive storytelling game, the player explores the role played by artificial intelligence in modifying human identity and actions in the system of assessment. Set in an interview conducted by an artificial intelligence system, this game requires players to act in accordance with certain invisible standards, including efficiency, obedience, and predictability. The core idea behind The Perfect Candidate is the possibility of human sincerity and authenticity in the context of reward for conformity.

The important decisions in this game come from the options available to the player of being either authentic or strategic in their answers. For example, choosing creativity over efficiency earns a player subtle negative feedback, whereas acting productively and efficiently is praised. It is ultimately a player’s choice of questioning the authority of an artificial intelligence or accepting it that decides on the end result

The mechanics of the game are designed in a way that supports procedural rhetoric by making an argument in the rules. The player will only be able to succeed when following the preferences of the AI, thus indirectly undermining any form of independence or critical thinking. Instead of openly stating that AI systems can manipulate people’s behavior, this game shows the player how it is done by forcing him to follow through with the requirements.

By having a small number of successful endings, the game highlights the problems associated with relying too much on AI. While AI helps optimize many aspects of life, its implementation often comes at a price - autonomy and authorship.

Artist Statement: 

In The Perfect Candidate, I learned that games are a system of creating meaning through the process of interaction instead of the process of narrativization.While in traditional story writing it is possible to present thematic elements of a game via the story and its dialogue, in making games, one needs to think about ways in which rules and restrictions create the game argument through allowing certain things.

One of the most difficult aspects of this project was thinking about meaningful choices within a game in such a way that they could fit into the argument that was being made. Instead of simply having different paths that players can take, every decision that a player makes needs to contribute to the bigger theme.

While the use of AI in this project was supportive, it was limited. The tool proved to be helpful in coming up with ideas and structuring them appropriately. However, there were times when the generated material lacked the intentionality required for articulating an argument clearly. Therefore, I had to scrutinize and correct the recommendations from the tool to ensure that the output conformed to my point of view. Thus, this project reiterated the significance of exercising human discretion when using technologies capable of producing materials rapidly.

In case I expanded this project in the future, I would introduce other aspects, including dynamic variables that monitor players’ actions and modify the game accordingly. Generally, this project enabled me to understand that games are important for conducting critical analyses of current topics, such as the influence of AI on human choices.
