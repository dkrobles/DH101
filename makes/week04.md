In this comic, The Holy Grail of Pizza, I wanted to turn a simple goal–finding the best slice in New York City–into an epic quest. By framing Durk and Duke’s pizza search as a “holy grail,” I exaggerated something ordinary and made it feel legendary. The structure mirrors a classic adventure narrative: the call to action (NYC Streets), guidance from mentors (Tips from Locals), obstacles (The Final Stretch), revelation (The Legendary Pizzeria) and reward (Celebration Time).

Using the comic format allowed me to visualize movement and chaos in ways a standard essay could not. The busy streets, subway platform, and glowing pizzeria aren’t just settings–they create rhythm. Each panel moves the characters forward physically while also building anticipation. Visually, the city becomes both a playground and a challenge, reinforcing how urban spaces shape experience.

This project connects to digital humanities by blending storytelling, visual design, and cultural identity. Pizza in NYC isn’t just food; it’s a tradition, community, and myth. By making two characters navigate distractions and advice, I highlight how meaning is built through exploration. The comic shows that even everyday experiences can become heroic when framed through narrative and perspective.

AI is a collaborator, tool, and plagiarist in various ways depending on how it's implemented: while it could serve as a collaborator, tool, and sometimes plagiarist regarding its use. It acts much like a word processor or thesaurus, both enhancing your efficiency as a tool. It collaborates by perhaps giving an outlook or dialogue that the person wouldn't have considered, hence enabling a different way to co-create worlds and characters together. It can turn into a plagiarist if it offers content too close to already existing things without actually showing originality. There are other ethical dilemmas with it, such as originality and intellectually property. Yet again, AI is inherently none of these; it's a reflection of intention and care on the human side of it. When used responsibly, AI enhances storytelling rather than taking its place, blending human creativity with technological assistance.
