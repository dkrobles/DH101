Research Documentation:
The project explores the global infrastructure of AI through a mapping of the headquarters, data centers, and mining sites of corporations. The main idea was to visualize the physical systems that support AI and highlight the dependency of the technology on the global network of resources, labor, and infrastructure.

Several sources were used to find the locations and understand their importance. Sources used for the research include reports by the AI Now Institute, as well as the book “Atlas of AI” by Kate Crawford, which discusses the dependency of AI systems on physical infrastructure and natural resources. Further information about the company headquarters and AI companies was obtained from online sources, including reports about the major AI companies OpenAI and Anthropic.

The research conducted on data centers showed that they were set up in areas where there are cheap land, good internet connectivity, and availability of electricity. Northern Virginia is one of the leading data centers in the world due to the availability of internet connectivity and fiber-optic cables. Oregon and Iowa are also preferred sites for data centers due to the availability of renewable energy and tax holidays for technology companies.

One of the hardest parts of this research was finding out what the entire supply chain was for AI hardware. Places where mining takes place for materials like cobalt, lithium, and other precious materials, which are necessary for building computers that run AI technologies, are rarely ever talked about in tech news. These materials are mined in countries that are nowhere near the corporate office where AI technologies are being created.

One of the most unexpected findings was the geographical distribution of the AI system. While
the media tends to talk about Silicon Valley, the actual hardware and infrastructure of AI is
spread across the globe. Mining countries in Africa, South America, and Asia, data centers
providing the necessary computing power, and corporate offices managing the investment and
development.

Mapping these locations helps reveal that AI is not just digital software but a global network of energy systems, minerals, infrastructure, and corporate decision-making.


## Reflection
Respond to this week’s reflection prompt in 200–300 words.

The map illustrates the invisible world infrastructure that enables the existence of AI.Although AI is often viewed as a technology that is “in the cloud” and is therefore digital, the physical location represented in the map shows the existence of physical world infrastructure that enables the existence of AI, including corporate power, computer infrastructure, and natural resources.

A notable trend is visible if one considers all these locations at once. For instance, the corporate offices of major tech firms, which control the direction of AI development, investment, and profit, tend to be located in wealthy parts of the United States, particularly California and the Pacific Northwest. On the other hand, the physical infrastructure that enables AI is dispersed around the world. For instance, data centers require massive amounts of electrical power and water for cooling computer servers that power the massive AI models. Some of the data centers use the same amount of power as tens of thousands of households, as well as billions of gallons of water for cooling systems.

Another important but invisible component of the infrastructure of AI is the mining sites. These require minerals such as lithium, cobalt, and rare earth elements, which are used in computer hardware, batteries, and server components used in AI. The extraction of these resources has a negative impact on the environment.

Inspired by the ideas of data feminism, this map seeks to make visible systems that are usually hidden behind technological innovation. By illustrating the location of AI infrastructure, the map argues that AI is not purely a virtual entity, but rather a global system affected by economic power, environmental resources, and material extraction.
