Research Notes:
Sources that were utilized within this paper include “Behind the Screen” by Sarah T. Roberts, as well as the “The Trauma Floor” article by Casey Newton from The Verge magazine and the TIME news investigation about Facebook content moderators.

One of the most challenging aspects regarding this topic is obtaining accurate information regarding wages. Since many moderators are hired by outside contractors, it is not always clear how much money they earn. Moreover, companies do not disclose this information because of the outsourced nature of their job.

The most surprising aspect for me was the intensity of psychological consequences. Moderators have to deal with horrific images and videos of sexual abuse, violent death, and other disturbing materials, yet their workload is expected to be highly productive despite such conditions. Numerous cases of PTSD and other serious psychological problems among moderators were reported. It was also shocking to see how deliberately hidden this work process is made by social media companies, who advertise themselves as having purely artificial intelligence technologies.

This leads us to the notion of “hidden labor” and the system of Hicks with the existence of invisible workers. Moderation demonstrates how the use of artificial intelligence is only partly automated and needs underpaid human laborers to operate well. The product created–social networks that can be safely used–is mostly used by huge tech companies, while the hardest work is not remunerated accordingly.

Design Statement:
In deciding on a digital tool for my project, I opted for a Canva zine since it would allow me to achieve a blend of visual storytelling and informative content. This medium communication can be used to dissect complicated matters in bite-size chunks while keeping the human element alive.

For my particular audience, I am targeting social media users who take everything that happens in their online accounts for granted. More specifically, there are social media users out there who think that all content moderation in social media platforms is performed by machines alone. They are not aware that the work is done by people behind the scenes.

Through the use of high levels of contrast, (clean vs. disturbing visuals), the core message is
emphasized: that what we see on the web has been curated through invisible labor. Large figures and declarative statements have been used to grab people’s attention, and shorter paragraphs have been included to make the content accessible and interesting to read.

From an emotional perspective, I hope that the users will feel uncomfortable but aware of the message being conveyed–and hopefully take responsibility for it. The call to action invites the users to question the systems that they interact within and become aware of the people behind these systems.
