GPT Literary Analysis:
This passage from The Raven explores themes of grief, psychological torment, and the human search for meaning in loss. The speaker, alone at midnight, mourns the death of Lenore, a woman he describes as “rare and radiant.” The gloomy setting—“midnight dreary” and “bleak December”—immediately establishes a dark, melancholic atmosphere that mirrors the narrator’s emotional state.

A central symbol in the poem is the Raven, which enters the chamber and repeatedly speaks the word “Nevermore.” The bird functions as both a literal presence and a symbolic representation of the narrator’s despair. Each time the Raven responds, the narrator projects deeper meaning onto its single word, interpreting it as confirmation that he will never escape his grief or reunite with Lenore. This reveals how the speaker’s own mind intensifies his suffering.

Poe also uses repetition, rhythm, and sound devices—especially internal rhyme and alliteration—to create a hypnotic, almost obsessive tone. Phrases like “rapping, rapping” and the repeated refrain “Nevermore” reinforce the narrator’s mental spiral. As the poem progresses, the speaker shifts from curiosity to desperation and finally to hopelessness.

Ultimately, the Raven symbolizes inescapable memory and permanent loss. The final image of the bird casting a shadow over the narrator suggests that his grief will permanently dominate his mind, leaving his soul trapped in sorrow “nevermore” to be lifted.


## Reflection
Respond to this week’s reflection prompt in 200–300 words.

While reading literature by machine, we create new ways of viewing the patterns in the text, but we miss out on the deeper emotional interpretation that comes with reading it. For instance, Voyant helps us view the patterns that might not be immediately apparent. For example, in the Voyant visualization, we can see that words like “nevermore”, “door”, “chamber”, and “raven” appear repeatedly in the poem. From the trends graph, we can see that certain words appear more frequently in certain parts of the text. This shows us how the poem uses repetition to create the theme of obsession in the narrator.

However, Voyant does not understand the emotional and symbolic content of the words. It can understand that the word “nevermore” is repeated many times, but it cannot understand that the word symbolizes the despair of the narrator and the finality of the loss. This is the point at which the GPT tool differs in understanding the text. GPT understands the theme, symbols, and tone of the text.

As a result of this, the two tools, Voyant and GPT, have different ways of imagining the concept of meaning. For example, Voyant imagines meaning in terms of patterns and data, while GPT imagines meaning in terms of interpretation and narrative significance. These two tools, therefore, demonstrate that machine reading can be useful in emphasizing both the patterns and the interpretations of the text, although they cannot be entirely relied upon for the purposes of gaining insight.
