## Reflection
Respond to this week’s reflection prompt in 200–300 words.

When an image repeats instead of standing still, it changes from a single moment into a small performance. A still image captures one instant, but a looping GIF creates motion and rhythm. In the video, the dancing cat’s movements repeat in a cycle, which makes the scene feel playful and slightly hypnotic. Instead of focusing on one frame, the viewer starts to notice the pattern of movement–the paws swinging, the body bouncing, and the tail moving with the beat. The repetition turns the image into something closer to a short dance routine than a simple picture.

Looping also affects attention and emotion. Because the movement repeats, the viewer keeps watching to catch small details they might have missed before. The loop makes the action feel continuous and almost endless, which adds humor and lightness to the scene. The repeated dance makes the cat appear energetic and entertaining, even though the same few seconds are playing again and again.

The element of human choice is seen in the choices the creator makes to create the GIF. These choices involve the prompt, the style, and the timing. The choices involve the subject of the image, the clothing, and the framing. Automation is seen in the creation of the animation. The creation of the animation, the movement, the textures, and the environment are all done by the AI.

The element of AI that makes the concept of authorship and originality complex is the fact that the image created is a mix of a human’s idea and the creation of the AI. It is the idea of the human, but details are created by the AI.
