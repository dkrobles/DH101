# Week 6 Reflection

Reflect 6: What do we gain/lose when machines “read” literature for us?

By having machines "read" literature for us, we gain efficiency, speed, and new ways of analyzing literature. With the aid of machines, we can analyze thousands of books efficiently, spot patterns, and find connections that might be hard for the human eye to spot. This can be helpful for scholars who want to study literatue more efficiently, and it can also open new avenues for studying literature, especially for the digital humanities.

On the other hand, we also lose some essential qualities that come with the traditional form of reading. Literature, as we all know, is all about interpretation, emotion, and personal connections. When we let machines read literature for us, we might be losing the personal connections that come with it.
