# Week 1 Reflection

Reflect 1: What counts as “making” in the age of AI? What might be gained/lost when machines participate in making?

In the age of AI making things is not about doing it all by yourself. Now making things is also about telling technology what to do picking the ideas you like and deciding how AI should make them. Making things is more about thinking and being creative than just using your hands. People work together with machines than doing everything on their own and this is what making things is, like now.

AI allows more people to make art and music and write things and work on projects. This is really great because it means people don't need to know a lot of things to be creative. AI makes it easier for people to be creative and try things and come up with new ideas faster. AI helps people make art and music and write things because it's very good, at doing things that people aren't very good at. 

However, something can be lost. The process may feel less personal, and people may rely too much on machines instead of learning skills themselves. Creativity can become less original and more repetitive. Overall, AI changes making into a shared process where humans provide meaning and direction, while machines help with execution.
