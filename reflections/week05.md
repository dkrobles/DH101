# Week 5 Reflection

Reflect 5: How does AI alter authorship and remix culture? Who owns AI-made art?

AI transforms authoring by making it a collaborative effort between human and machine. Rather than authoring every word or image, the human may conceptualize the prompt, select the output, refine the output, and make the final call. This makes the notion of "author" as the sole source of creation obsolete. AI also speeds up the process of remixing. Since the models are trained on a massive amount of existing creative content, they can remix styles, genres, and references in an instant. Remixing becomes easier, but it also becomes problematic in terms of originality and influence.

The ownership of AI-generated art is still an area of legal and moral controversy. Since copyrighting requires human creativity, purely autonomous AI-generated art may not be eligible for copyrighting. Also, the ownership may depend on the level of human creativity involved. There are also questions about the rights of artists whose works are used in training AI models, making AI-generated art a very interesting convergence of creativity, labor, and law.
