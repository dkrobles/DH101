# Week 11 Reflection

Reflect 11: Who does the invisible work of AI, and who profits from it?

The invisible side of AI is performed by underpaid human workers, such as data annotators, content moderators, and crowd workers, mostly located in the Global South. They tag images, screen offensive content, and teach algorithms; however, their labor is concealed by the myth of the total automation of processes. Most often, these people earn meager salaries, receive no job guarantees, and come into contact with graphic and harmful materials without proper protection measures. At the same time, the main profit goes to huge tech corporations that operate AI software, their top management, and the owners of their shares. These enterprises enjoy high efficiency, scale of operations, and competitiveness. Therefore, an imbalance exists between the people who perform important but challenging tasks without recognition and reward and big enterprises that accumulate almost all benefits.
